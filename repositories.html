AI Governance Stack Repositories

Canonical repository map for the kannanokannan open-source AI governance stack.

The stack is organized around a simple premise: probabilistic intelligence becomes production-ready only when it is placed inside deterministic governance boundaries, explicit context handling, and operational controls.

Core Repositories

  • ContextOps Operational layer for context-aware AI systems.

    ContextOps focuses on the practical management of context in production: context flow, context lifecycle, operational handoffs, and the controls needed to make model behavior observable and governable.

  • ContextBoundary Deterministic boundary layer around probabilistic AI behavior.

    ContextBoundary defines the constraints, checks, and governance interfaces that keep AI behavior inside explicit limits. It is the guardrail layer for systems where model output must remain accountable.

  • Sthala Structural layer for governed AI execution spaces.

    Sthala represents the place or structure in which governed AI activity occurs. It frames how components, contexts, and boundaries relate to each other inside an AI governance architecture.

  • Griha Application layer for usable governed AI systems.

    Griha is the layer where governed AI capabilities become usable products or workflows. It connects the underlying governance architecture to practical user-facing systems.

  • context-stack Integrated stack tying the governance components together.

    context-stack acts as the integration point for the broader architecture. It connects ContextOps, ContextBoundary, Sthala, and Griha into a coherent stack for context-governed AI systems.
